Building a marketing function from scratch

Safe Money is a Canadian personal-finance app built on a psychology-first idea: budgeting fails because of mental load, not maths. I led marketing — setting the strategy, hiring and running the content team, and building the system that took the brand from no marketing at all to a live multi-channel launch.

The situation

Safe Money had a product and a point of view, but no marketing function — no positioning, no content, no channels running, and no one producing. The brand was heading toward a relaunch with nothing to launch with.

The job wasn't to run a campaign. It was to build the thing that runs campaigns: work out who the product was for, decide what the brand stood for, hire the people, put a production system around them, and get content shipping on a reliable cadence — then plan the paid layer on top of it.

01

Strategy

Before anything could be produced, the brand needed a reason to be chosen. I ran the market and competitor analysis, and landed on a position that didn't compete on features — "Money Without The Mental Load" — then built the audience, pillar, channel and budget framework the whole team executed against.

03

Paid acquisition

Paid was deliberately gated behind organic results — no spend until engagement data showed which creative worked, so budget amplified proven content instead of guessing. I modelled the budget backwards from CPI estimates and structured it as a test-then-scale split, so the majority of spend followed real performance rather than an estimate.

Where it landed

  • Built the marketing function from zero — positioning, personas, pillars and channel plan where none existed.
  • Stood up a production system — brand guidebook and SOPs that let a two-person team produce without me in every loop.
  • Shipped a live multi-channel relaunch week — planned, written and scheduled across four channels.
  • Scoped the paid layer — media plan, CPI model and test-and-scale framework, ready to launch behind organic validation.
  • Honest limit: the paid campaign had not gone live during my time on it, so this case study shows planning and organic execution — not paid results.
